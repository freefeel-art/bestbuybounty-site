GR2 Glycemic Response Control Technology

The Science Behind GR2: Why Glycemic Response Matters for Weight

The GR2 framework is built on a well-established body of nutritional research: blood sugar regulation is a primary driver of hunger, fat storage, and sustained energy. When blood sugar rises rapidly — as it does after high-glycemic meals — the pancreas releases insulin to manage it. High insulin levels suppress fat burning, promote fat storage, and trigger the blood sugar crash that drives cravings and overeating.

Managing glycemic response — slowing and moderating blood sugar rise after meals — creates conditions where the body can more effectively use stored fat for energy, maintain more stable hunger signals, and sustain better energy levels throughout the day. This is not a novel hypothesis: it is the mechanistic basis behind low-glycemic dietary approaches that have been studied in peer-reviewed research for decades.

GR2 Glycemic Response Control Technology applies this principle through the strategic use of protein (which has minimal glycemic impact and promotes satiety), fibre, and specific nutrient timing to manage blood sugar response at the meal level.

NeoLifeShake: The Protein Quality Standard

The centrepiece of the NeoFit90 program is NeoLifeShake — a meal replacement providing 18g of protein in 160 calories. Two metrics matter most when evaluating meal replacement protein quality: protein quantity per calorie, and protein completeness.

PDCAAS Above 1.0: What It Means

The PDCAAS (Protein Digestibility Corrected Amino Acid Score) is the standard measure of protein quality used by nutrition regulatory bodies worldwide. A score of 1.0 represents the maximum — it means the protein provides all essential amino acids in the proportions required by the human body and is fully digestible.

NeoLifeShake has a PDCAAS score above 1.0 — placing it on par with egg white and whey protein, the reference standards for complete protein. For a 160-calorie meal replacement, this represents exceptional protein quality density.
