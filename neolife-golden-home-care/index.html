The Founding Principle: Concentrate, Don't Ship Water

When you buy a ready-mixed cleaning product from a retail shelf, typically 90–98% of what you transport home and eventually discard is water. NeoLife's home care line was built on a different principle from the outset in the 1960s: deliver the active cleaning chemistry in concentrated form, and let the user add tap water at home. This eliminates the environmental and economic cost of shipping, storing, and packaging what is, in effect, labelled tap water.

LDC mixes 1 part concentrate to 5 parts water — one litre makes 6 litres of ready-to-use cleaner. Super 10 mixes 1:10 — one litre makes 11 litres. The U.S. EPA's Safer Choice program estimates that concentrate formats reduce plastic packaging by up to 70% per cleaning task compared to ready-to-use products.

LDC: Light Duty Concentrate

LDC is a neutral-pH, phosphate-free, 100% biodegradable multi-purpose cleaner. The neutral pH makes it appropriate for surfaces sensitive to alkaline or acid exposure. Its surfactant system holds oils and grease in suspension so they drain away cleanly — eliminating the ring-around-the-sink that typically requires a separate cleanser product.

Uses: Kitchen surfaces and food preparation areas, hand dishwashing, delicate fabric hand-washing, fruit and vegetable washing, jewellery cleaning, waterless hand cleaning, bathroom surfaces, floor cleaning.

Environmental Credentials

Three environmental claims are central to the Golden Home Care line, each with specific technical meaning. Phosphate-free means rinse water does not introduce phosphate nutrients into waterways — a concern codified in EU Regulation No 259/2012. NeoLife's formulations predate these regulations by decades. 100% biodegradable means active surfactant ingredients break down by microbial action. Concentrate format means dramatically reduced packaging waste and transport emissions per cleaning dose.

Super 10 has been used in wildlife rescue operations including cleaning oil-soaked penguins in South Africa. At the Bordano House of Butterflies in Friuli, Italy, Super 10 and LDC are the only cleaning products used — chosen for their safety profile in sensitive ecological environments.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is NeoLife Golden Home Care truly biodegradable?

Yes. NeoLife states that 100% of the active surfactant ingredients are biodegradable — broken down by microbial action into water, carbon dioxide, and mineral compounds. The phosphate-free formulation additionally prevents phosphate nutrient pollution in waterways.

How much does one bottle of Super 10 actually yield?

One litre of Super 10 concentrate yields approximately 11 litres of ready-to-use cleaner at standard 1:10 dilution. NeoLife characterises a single bottle as equivalent to more than 20 standard-size retail cleaning bottles in cleaning tasks completed.

What is the difference between LDC and Super 10?

LDC uses a neutral-pH surfactant system for everyday cleaning — dishes, general surfaces, delicate fabrics, food preparation areas. Super 10 is heavier-duty with triple-action chemistry (penetrate, dissolve, emulsify) for grease, floors, tiles, and intensive cleaning. Both are phosphate-free, 100% biodegradable, and concentrated.

How does this relate to Nordic kemikaliebantning?

The Nordic kemikaliebantning movement encourages replacing synthetic household chemicals with biodegradable, low-impact alternatives. The Golden Home Care line meets these criteria structurally — phosphate-free, biodegradable, concentrate format — having been formulated on these principles in the 1960s before the movement existed.
