What Is Pro Vitality+ and Why Four Components?

The foundational premise of Pro Vitality+ is that nutritional sufficiency cannot be achieved by addressing micronutrient gaps alone. The SAB's research framework identifies four distinct cellular nutrition gaps that conventional multivitamins do not address: the depletion of grain-derived lipids and sterols from refined modern diets, widespread carotenoid insufficiency due to low fruit and vegetable consumption, near-universal omega-3 deficiency in non-fish-eating populations, and micronutrient shortfalls across vitamins and minerals. Addressing one gap without the others leaves the cellular environment partially optimized. Pro Vitality+ was designed to close all four simultaneously, in a single daily packet.

The cellular nutrition philosophy underlying the system holds that the effectiveness of any nutrient depends on the health of the cell membrane through which it must pass. If cell membranes are rigid due to lipid and sterol depletion — a consequence of modern grain refining that removes the lipid fraction — even adequate vitamin and mineral intake is partially wasted because transport efficiency is compromised. Tre-en-en addresses this foundational layer first. Carotenoid Complex and Salmon Oil Plus then provide the phytonutrient and fatty acid environment that supports cell membrane function and immune response. The Essential Vitamin & Mineral Complex completes the system at the micronutrient level, using plant-sourced phytoenzymes to support absorption.

The Problem with Standard Multivitamins

A standard multivitamin delivers isolated vitamins and minerals in a single tablet. What it does not deliver is the lipid and sterol fraction from whole grains (removed during refining), the broad-spectrum carotenoid complex from a diverse range of fruits and vegetables, or the full omega-3 profile from marine sources. These are not micronutrients — they are functional food components that operate at the cell membrane level. No standard multivitamin addresses them because they require separate, purpose-built delivery formats: softgels for lipid-soluble components, concentrated whole-food extracts for carotenoids, and molecularly tested fish oil for omega-3s.

The Research Behind Each Component

Tre-en-en Grain Concentrates (1958) is the world's first phytonutrient supplement — developed by NeoLife before the term "phytonutrient" existed in scientific literature. It delivers the lipid and sterol fraction removed from grains during modern milling: wheat germ, rice bran oil, and soy. A 1987 study at Texas A&M University compared animals fed a standard diet against those receiving the Tre-en-en lipid fraction. Despite identical caloric intake, the Tre-en-en group showed improved growth, more developed cardiovascular systems, and higher adrenal function — interpreted as evidence of improved nutrient utilization efficiency at the cellular level.

Carotenoid Complex has the most extensive independent research record of the four components. A 1994 study in the American Journal of Clinical Nutrition (Carughi & Hooper, PMID: 8147336) was the first to confirm serum uptake of multiple carotenoids from a whole-food supplement — not just beta-carotene. USDA-partnered clinical research documented a 37% increase in immune capacity over 20 days, with specific enhancement of natural killer (NK) cell and lymphocyte activity. A placebo-controlled, double-blind study published in the Journal of the American College of Nutrition measured normalization of MDA-TBA lipid peroxidation markers.

Salmon Oil Plus delivers all eight omega-3 fatty acids — not only EPA and DHA — and has been tested against more than 200 potential contaminants using a zero-tolerance purity standard. Clinical research on the formula has documented a 17% reduction in triglycerides, a 38% increase in the omega-3 index, and a 68% reduction in an inflammatory marker.

Essential Vitamin & Mineral Complex

The fourth component delivers 21 vitamins and minerals alongside plant-sourced phytoenzymes designed to support absorption. Its heritage traces to NeoLife's Formula IV, developed in the 1950s with SAB oversight. The inclusion of phytoenzymes reflects the same whole-food philosophy applied across the Pro Vitality+ system: the bioavailability of micronutrients depends on the enzymatic and co-factor environment in which they are delivered, not only on the milligram amounts listed on a label.
