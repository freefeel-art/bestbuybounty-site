What Is Tre-en-en Grain Concentrates?

Tre-En-En® Grain Concentrates is a dietary supplement providing concentrated extracts of lipids and sterols from three whole-food sources: wheat germ, rice bran, and soybeans. It was developed by NeoLife and introduced in 1958, making it the world's first phytonutrient supplement — a category that would not become widely recognized in nutrition science until decades later.

The name derives from the Greek for "3-in-1," reflecting its three-source composition. Tre-en-en forms the foundation of NeoLife's Formula IV® multifactor supplement system and is developed under the oversight of NeoLife's Scientific Advisory Board.

The product is described on its label as providing "lipids and sterols for cellular nutrition and energy." Its underlying premise is that modern food processing removes nutrient-dense outer layers from dietary staple grains — and that this loss of phyto-lipids and phyto-sterols has measurable consequences for cellular function.

Why Grain Processing Created a Nutritional Gap

To extend shelf life, grains undergo approximately two dozen processes before becoming the white flour and white rice that form the basis of most modern diets. These processes remove the outer bran and germ layers — the parts of the grain that contain the majority of vitamins, minerals, and crucially, lipids and sterols.

"Processing removes the nutritious outer layers of the grain. These layers contain the majority of grain's vitamins and minerals, as well as 'good fats' called lipids and sterols." — NeoLife Tre-en-en Fast Facts, May 2025

The modern diet compounds this problem: artificial and hydrogenated fats have increasingly displaced natural plant fats in processed foods since the mid-20th century, altering the lipid and sterol profile of the average diet. Peer-reviewed research published in Biological Reviews (Hulbert et al., 2005) notes that dietary fat composition directly influences cellular membrane composition, which in turn affects metabolic function.

In the 1950s, a series of studies conducted at a Southern California hospital indicated that people could benefit from a mixture of plant extracts providing broad-spectrum lipids and sterols. This research formed part of the scientific basis for the original Tre-en-en formulation.

The Science Behind Lipids and Sterols

Every cell in the human body is surrounded by a lipid membrane. This membrane controls what enters and exits the cell — nutrients in, waste and metabolic products out. When the membrane lacks adequate lipids and sterols, cells become less efficient: they can struggle to absorb nutrients even when those nutrients are available, and they can accumulate waste products that healthy cells would export normally.

The plant sterols in Tre-en-en — beta-sitosterol, gamma-oryzanol, stigmasterol, campesterol, and octacosanol — are naturally present in the bran and outer grain layers removed during processing. Research on rice bran oil specifically has documented anti-inflammatory effects and improvements in mitochondrial respiration in controlled studies (Lee et al., PLoS One, 2019).

Who Uses Tre-en-en

Tre-en-en is used by people who are specifically interested in cellular nutrition — the idea that health starts at the level of the individual cell, and that cell membrane function depends on adequate lipid and sterol intake. This is a narrower and more specific concern than general vitamin supplementation, and it tends to attract people who have already researched the role of dietary fats in cellular health.

A second audience is people already using the NeoLife Pro Vitality+ system who want a standalone supply of the Tre-en-en component — either because they want to adjust their dosage independently or because they are exploring NeoLife's product line systematically.

The product is relevant to anyone whose diet is heavily based on processed grain staples (white bread, white rice, refined flour products), as these are precisely the foods from which the lipids and sterols that Tre-en-en provides have been removed.

How to Use Tre-en-en

The suggested use, as stated on the product label, is 1 to 3 softgels daily with meals. The standard serving size used in the Supplement Facts panel is 3 softgels (one full serving), which delivers 2025 mg of the Grain Concentrate Blend.

Taking the softgels with meals is recommended because the lipid-soluble components — phyto-sterols and omega fatty acids — are absorbed more efficiently in the presence of dietary fat. Each container holds 120 softgels, providing 40 servings at the 3-softgel serving size.

Tre-en-en should be stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. The product includes a safety seal.
