The Omega-3 Gap: Why It Matters

Long-chain omega-3 fatty acids — primarily EPA and DHA — are among the most extensively researched nutrients in clinical nutrition. Yet omega-3 deficiency remains one of the most prevalent nutritional gaps in industrialized populations. The primary source of EPA and DHA is cold-water fatty fish — salmon, mackerel, sardines — which most people in industrialized nations consume far too rarely. Plant-based omega-3 sources (flaxseed, walnuts) provide ALA, which the body converts to EPA and DHA at below 10% efficiency — insufficient to close the gap.

Why Purity Is the Central Challenge

Cold-water fish sit near the top of the marine food chain and bioaccumulate lipid-soluble environmental contaminants including methylmercury, PCBs, dioxins, and organochlorine pesticide residues. Independent testing of commercial fish oil supplements has repeatedly found contamination levels that vary by orders of magnitude between products with similar label claims.

Salmon Oil Plus is screened against more than 200 potential contaminants with a zero-tolerance philosophy. This panel includes heavy metals, the full spectrum of PCB congeners, dioxins, furans, brominated flame retardants, and organochlorine pesticide residues — going substantially beyond standard food safety testing requirements.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does Salmon Oil Plus deliver eight omega-3 fatty acids instead of just EPA and DHA?

Cold-water salmon contains a full spectrum of long-chain omega-3s — including DPA, SDA, ETA, ALA, HPA, and additional variants — each with distinct metabolic roles. The health outcomes associated with high fish consumption in epidemiological research reflect the complete omega-3 profile of the food source, not EPA and DHA in isolation.

What does "screened against 200+ contaminants" actually mean?

Every batch is tested against a panel of more than 200 potential environmental contaminants before release — including heavy metals, the full spectrum of PCB congeners, dioxins, furans, brominated flame retardants, and organochlorine pesticide residues. This goes substantially beyond standard food safety testing requirements.

Can I get enough omega-3 from flaxseed instead of fish oil?

Plant-based omega-3 sources provide ALA — a short-chain omega-3 that the body must convert to EPA and DHA. Human conversion efficiency from ALA to EPA is estimated below 10%, and conversion to DHA is substantially lower. Plant-based omega-3 sources cannot substitute for direct long-chain EPA and DHA intake from marine sources.
