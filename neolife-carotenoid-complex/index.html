The 1994 Bioavailability Study

The foundational peer-reviewed study on Carotenoid Complex was published in the American Journal of Clinical Nutrition in April 1994 (Carughi & Hooper, PMID: 8147336). The study used a depletion-repletion design: participants consumed a low-carotenoid diet for two weeks, then supplemented with Carotenoid Complex for four weeks. Plasma carotenoid concentrations were measured by HPLC. Within one week of supplementation, alpha- and beta-carotene returned to baseline levels. By the end of four weeks, both had risen significantly above pre-depletion baseline values (p < 0.05).

"This is the first study reporting increments of serum carotenoids, other than beta-carotene, after supplementation." — Carughi & Hooper, American Journal of Clinical Nutrition, 1994

The USDA Research Program: Immunity and Oxidative Protection

USDA researchers documented an approximately 37% increase in immune capacity over a 20-day supplementation period. The research measured enhanced activity in natural killer (NK) cells and lymphocytes — two components of the immune system that respond to cellular threats. Critically, the research also compared the broad-spectrum Carotenoid Complex against beta-carotene supplementation alone, finding that the mixed carotenoid formula produced greater immune benefits than beta-carotene in isolation.
