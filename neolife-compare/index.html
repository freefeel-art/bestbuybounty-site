The Concentrate Principle: Why You Pay for Water at the Store

When you buy a ready-mixed cleaning product from a supermarket shelf, a significant portion of what you are paying for — and transporting home — is water. The active cleaning ingredients represent a fraction of the bottle's volume. The rest is tap water with a markup.

NeoLife's home care products are concentrates. You buy the active ingredients. You add your own tap water at home. This is not a recent sustainability innovation — NeoLife has operated on the concentrate principle since the 1960s, before environmental marketing became standard. The consequence is a dramatically lower cost per cleaning dose, and significantly less plastic packaging per year of household use.
